## Design Context

### Users
LLCAtlas is for first-time solo founders and side-hustlers forming their first LLC in the United States. The core reader is often searching late at night for practical help with queries like "how to start an LLC in Ohio" and is anxious about making an expensive mistake.

They are not experienced operators, agency owners, serial founders, or non-US founders. They are overwhelmed by legal jargon, skeptical of upsells, and looking for one clear recommendation they can trust.

The primary jobs to be done are:
- Understand the real filing process in plain English.
- Compare formation options without getting manipulated by teaser pricing.
- Decide what to do next with confidence and minimal cognitive load.

The interface should leave them feeling confident, relieved, and able to take action without second-guessing every step.

### Brand Personality
The brand voice is plainspoken, protective, and decisive.

LLCAtlas should feel like a sharp, trustworthy guide who has already done the messy comparison work and is willing to say what is worth paying for and what is not. The tone should be calm and direct, never theatrical, never salesy, and never dressed up to feel more "professional" than it is.

### Aesthetic Direction
The intended visual direction is a modern consumer guide with a clean editorial feel. The closest reference point is the clarity and recommendation-first usefulness of Wirecutter, adapted for LLC formation research.

The design should:
- Prioritize readability, hierarchy, and clarity over visual novelty.
- Stay light-only, with no dark mode.
- Preserve the existing warm orange or amber CTA language as the primary accent.
- Rely mostly on white and neutral grays for structure and surfaces.

Anti-references:
- Fake law-firm luxury: no marble, no faux-premium gravitas, no navy-and-gold legal theatrics.
- Generic fintech SaaS: no gradient blobs, abstract startup illustrations, or empty aspirational marketing language.
- Government portal blandness: no lifeless bureaucratic stiffness.
- Corporate media density: no content-farm sprawl, ad-like clutter, or overpacked comparison layouts.

Color constraints:
- Keep warm orange or amber as the action color.
- Avoid blue because of LegalZoom brand association.
- Avoid green because of ZenBusiness association.
- Keep the palette restrained and clean rather than colorful.

Accessibility and readability constraints:
- Meet standard WCAG AA expectations.
- Favor large, readable body text with a 16px minimum and 18px preferred where appropriate.
- Use generous line height and calm spacing because users are anxious first-timers reading carefully, not scanning like power users.

### Design Principles
1. Recommendation first. The design should quickly surface the verdict, the next step, and the reason behind it.
2. Reduce anxiety through clarity. Every layout, label, and content grouping should make the process feel simpler and more manageable.
3. Protect the reader from noise. Avoid decorative patterns, dense UI, and anything that feels like an upsell funnel or content farm.
4. Earn trust with restraint. Use a clean light editorial system, restrained color, and direct language instead of faux authority or startup gloss.
5. Optimize for careful reading. Favor readable type, strong hierarchy, generous spacing, and obvious action paths over novelty.
